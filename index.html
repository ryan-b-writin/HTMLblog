Video game consoles: My top 5

My five favorite video game consoles, in no particular order:

Super Nintendo

You don't title a post like this and not put the Super Nintendo
at the top. Even if you aren't ranking them, even if you're
only showing the post to classmates. Someone on the internet will
KNOW, immediately, and they will find you.

The Super Nintendo represents one of video games' most iconic and
well-known game producers at the peak of their power. Not only
were its' games leaps and bounds ahead of the competition
technologically, but many were so well-made that they're still fun
to play to this day.

5 excellent games:

  • Super Mario World
  • Earthbound
  • Chrono Trigger
  • Super Metroid
  • Mega Man X

By: Ryan B, 1/12/16

Nintendo 3DS

The Nintendo 3DS is Nintendo's latest and greatest handheld console.
Nintendo handhelds trace a fine lineage alllll the way back to the
OG "brick" Game Boy. The 3DS being the most powerful of these, it's
no surprise that it boasts some of Nintendo's very best games period.
In addition, a wifi antenna makes online play possible- which is great
if you write blogs about your favorite game consoles but never have
anyone to play with!

5 excellent games:

  • Super Mario 3D Land
  • Super Smash Bros. For Nintendo 3DS
  • Kid Icarus: Uprising
  • Pokemon X or Pokemon Y
  • Shovel Knight (download only)

By: Ryan B, 1/12/16

Sega Genesis

Here's a bit of my own personal bias showing through. The Genesis
was the first game console I owned. Cliche as it sounds, I
mowed lawns for a whole summer to save up. The commercials for
Sonic the Hedgehog got to me, and their message still has hold
of me to this day: "Look how cool games are!!"

Despite being noticeably weaker than its direct competition, the
Super Nintendo, the thing you can say about the Genesis is that it
had style. Its games had an edgier look and feel, complete with grinding
guitar soundtracks to play them to- though I should point out that the
tech wasn't really there yet, and the 'guitars' usually sounded like alarm
clocks being ground up.

5 excellent games:

  • Sonic the Hedgehog 3 & Knuckles
  • Beyond Oasis
  • Toejam & Earl
  • Streets of Rage 2
  • Gunstar Heroes

By: Ryan B, 1/12/16

Sony Playstation 2

Sony entered the console market with the Playstation and it, like the
Super Nintendo before it, established Sony as the big power player
for a generation. The Playstation 2 followed and built on Sony's
lead, despite strong competition. Sony made a handful of great titles
in-house, but what made the Playstations stand out was the enormous
selection of third-party titles available.

The Playstation 2 will also play Playstation 1 games, so no need to choose
between them!

5 excellent games:

  • Castlevania: Symphony of the Night (PS1)
  • Katamari Damacy (PS2)
  • Breath of Fire III (PS1)
  • Soul Calibur 2 (PS2)
  • Brave Fencer Musashi (PS1)

By: Ryan B, 1/12/16

previous generation, and I give the edge to the 360- just barely- because
it has a sliiightly larger library of titles. Most of the big games
came out on both platforms, and there's plenty to recommend the PS3 as well.
